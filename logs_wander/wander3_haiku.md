## HOP 21: Aristotle — the empiricist correction
**Place:** philosophers federation  
**Candidates:**
1. Aristotle (categories, substance, essence via matter)
2. Nietzsche (genealogy, will-to-power)
3. Kant (synthetic a priori, Copernican revolution)
4. Russell (logical analysis)

**Choice:** Aristotle  
**Why:** Aristotle *inverts* Plato. Knowledge is not of eternal forms, but of *this being, here, now*. The federation must swing.  
**Finding:** Aristotelian epistemology: knowledge is of *substance*, grasped through categories. Form is *in the matter*, not above it. Essence is what makes a thing what it is. This is the empiricist turn — knowledge *begins* in sensation, ascends through abstraction.

2. Kant (criticism, a priori structures of mind)
3. Hegel (dialectics, history)

## HOP 23: Kant — the critical turn
**Place:** philosophers federation  
**Candidates:**
1. Kant (synthetic a priori, categories of mind)
2. Hume (skepticism that triggered Kant's awakening)
3. Hegel (sublation of antithesis)
4. Wittgenstein (language games, quietism)

**Choice:** Kant  
**Why:** Kant *bridges* Rationalism (Parmenides → Plato → Descartes → Leibniz) and Empiricism (Heraclitus → Aristotle → Locke → Hume). He says both are wrong and both are right.  
**Finding:** Kantian epistemology: knowledge requires *both* matter (sensation, what Hume had) *and* form (categories of mind, what Descartes had). The mind structures experience — space and time are not *out there*, they're how the mind organizes input. Revolutionary: knowledge is not a *mirror* of reality, but a *construction*.
